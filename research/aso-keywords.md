## App Store Metadata Fields

### App Name (30 chars — highest keyword weight)
Recommended: `Ebb: Symptom & Flare Tracker`

### Subtitle (30 chars — second highest weight)
Recommended: `Daily chronic illness log`

### Keyword Field (100 chars, comma-separated, no spaces — no repeating words from name/subtitle)
```
symptom,tracker,chronic,illness,flare,pain,diary,fatigue,log,fibromyalgia,lupus,EDS,POTS,daily
```

---

## Keyword Tiers

### Tier 1 — Core (high intent, moderate volume, competitive but winnable)
| Keyword | Competition | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `flare tracker` | Low | No dominant owner — clear opportunity |
| `chronic illness tracker` | Medium | Bearable ranks but doesn't fully own it |
| `symptom log` | Low-Medium | Functional, searchable |
| `fatigue tracker` | Low | Strong ME/CFS demand, underserved |
| `pain diary` | Medium | PainScale ranks but is stale |

### Tier 2 — Long-tail (condition-specific, low competition, highly loyal communities)
| Keyword | Community | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `fibromyalgia tracker` | r/Fibromyalgia (100k+) | No dominant simple app |
| `ME CFS tracker` | r/ME_CFS | Passionate, underserved community |
| `EDS tracker` | r/EhlersDanlos | Niche but loyal |
| `POTS tracker` | r/POTS | Guava mentioned here but it's complex |
| `lupus symptom tracker` | r/lupus | No clear winner |
| `brain fog journal` | Multiple chronic illness subs | Unique angle, low competition |
| `chronic pain diary` | r/ChronicPain | PainScale ranks but is 2022 |
| `flare journal` | General chronic illness | Low competition |

---

## Strategy

**Don't compete head-on with Bearable on `symptom tracker`** — Bearable has authority there. Win on:

1. **Long-tail condition keywords** — Bearable ranks for these but users comparing options will see "simpler" in your description and convert
2. **The word "simple" / "30 seconds"** in your description — Bearable reviewers literally search for a simpler alternative after trying it
3. **Screenshot strategy** — show the 30-second log experience in screenshot 1. Before/after: "This is all you do." That converts.
