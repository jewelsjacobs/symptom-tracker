# Bearable — Competitor Deep Dive

**Researched:** 2026-02-22 by Lens (market research agent)
**App Store URL:** https://apps.apple.com/us/app/bearable-symptom-tracker/id1482581097
**Rating:** ⭐4.8 / 5K ratings
**Users:** 900,000+
**Last updated:** Active (2026)
**Price:** Freemium → subscription

## What It Does
Full-featured chronic illness / symptom tracker. Tracks mood, pain, meds, diet, exercise, habits, sleep, custom symptoms. Syncs with Apple Health and Fitbit. Has correlation/insight engine. Doctor report export. Data encryption.

## Strength
- Dominant player. 900k users is serious traction.
- Covers almost every angle: physical, mental, lifestyle tracking
- Active development, responsive to community

## Weakness (confirmed by Reddit — multi-year pattern)
**The single biggest complaint: it's overwhelming.**

Direct quotes:
- "Bearable was a bit overwhelming" (r/ChronicIllness, May 2024)
- "Bearable is the most popular but it was too much of a time sink for me personally" (r/disability, Nov 2022)
- "I just really don't get on with the interface... I find that quite overwhelming as an autistic person" (r/ChronicIllness, Apr 2022)
- "too complicated to set up and use" (general complaint, multiple threads)

## Strategic Implication
Bearable owns the "feature-rich" end of the market. The "simple, quick, 30-second check-in" end is EMPTY. Julia's app should be positioned as the antidote to Bearable's complexity — not a competitor to it.

Positioning: **"Bearable, but in 30 seconds a day."**

Target user: person with brain fog, fatigue, or anxiety who WANTS to track but abandons complex apps within a week.
